THE SKETCH FINDS MOVEMENT

How a Small Studio Sparked Global Hope

Sketch Finds began with just a single box of acrylic paints, heavy plaster texture media, and a big dream. Founded by a passionate 12-year-old artist, the platform originally set out to solve a simple problem: providing genuine, hand-layered canvases to art lovers who were tired of boring, mass-produced wall prints.

But as the home studio filled with completed canvases, a larger purpose took root. Our young founder realized that millions of incredibly talented kids, teens, and underprivileged children living in orphanages have beautiful internal worlds to share—but completely lack the resources, art supplies, or a digital platform to voice them.

Today, Sketch Finds has grown from a personal art portfolio into a thriving social enterprise. By opening our doors to young creators and partnering directly with global child welfare NGOs, we are proving that the next generation doesn't just make art—we build communities and change lives.

Turning Creativity Into Care

At Sketch Finds, we believe every child possesses an innate creative spark. Through our dedicated NGO Art Initiative, our team coordinates with local child welfare organizations to supply high-quality canvases, brushes, and acrylic paint kits directly to orphanages.

When you acquire a piece from our NGO collections, you aren't just decorating an interior wall—you are providing vital educational tools, funding shelter resources, and proving to a vulnerable child that their voice has immense value to the world.
